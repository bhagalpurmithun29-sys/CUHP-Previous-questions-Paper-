# Executive Intelligence Guide

## 1. Introduction
This guide provides an overview of how the Executive Intelligence platform transforms underlying data into high-level strategic insights, focusing on the interplay between Goals, Initiatives, and Dashboards.

## 2. Managing Strategic Goals
* **Definition**: A Strategic Goal is a long-term objective (e.g., "Improve Academic Excellence").
* **Mapping**: Every goal must be mapped to at least one quantifiable KPI from the Data Warehouse (e.g., "Average Course Grade", "Curriculum Coverage Index").
* **Performance Targets**: Goals require specific targets (e.g., "Coverage Index > 90% by Q4"). The platform automatically colors the Goal Status (Green/Yellow/Red) based on real-time DW data.

## 3. Risk & Opportunity Detection
The Executive Platform does not calculate risk directly. It consumes events from the **Decision Intelligence** engine.
* When the Decision Engine detects a trend predicting a goal failure, it flags it as a "Strategic Risk".
* Analysts can view the "Heat Map" to see which departments are contributing most to the risk and execute "Drill-downs" to the specific underlying metrics.

## 4. Automation & AI Briefings
* The platform utilizes NLP (via the AI Platform) to generate human-readable summaries of complex dashboards. This ensures Board Members who are not data experts can quickly grasp the institutional standing.
